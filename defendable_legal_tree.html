▼ CLAIM TYPING
What type of claim is this?
03 · Claim types · 14

Heads of claim & immediate branches

Type the claim first. Denied boarding and ordinary technical / own-staff paths short-circuit; Montreal consequential loss escalates.

CL-01

Delay 3+ hours

Art 7 compensation if arrival delay ≥3h. Measure doors-open at final destination.

→ EC available? · U-7 gate · DT trees
CL-02

Cancellation (<14 days)

Full Art 7 unless extraordinary circumstances + reasonable measures.

→ EC available? · DT-16 · notice windows
CL-03 / CL-04

Cancellation notice bands

13–7 days and <7 days — strictest rerouting windows; reduced quantum if offered.

→ Art 8 offer check · U-9
CL-08

Early Departure

Schedule brought forward >1 hour within 14 days treated as cancellation.

→ Treat as Art 5 · EC / RM analysis
CL-05 · DT-15

Denied Boarding

Involuntary offload / overbooking — carrier always liable.

→ ALWAYS LIABLE · CONCEDE · Art 7/8/9
CL-06 · U-12

Downgrading

Lower class than booked. Distance-band refund. EC does not excuse.

→ Quantum only · no EC screen
CL-07 · U-13

Missed Connection

Through booking: delay at final destination; distance from first departure.

→ Folkerts · first-leg operating carrier
CL-10 · U-10

Art 9 Care

Meals, communications, hotel/transport. Independent of EC outcome.

→ Care compliance · HOTAC pull
CL-11 · U-9

Art 8 Rerouting

Three options mandatory for cancellations and 5+ hour delays.

→ Offer evidenced? · standalone head
CL-09 · U-11

Montreal Art 19

Consequential / commercial loss. Art 22 SDR cap. Absolute 2-year bar.

→ ESCALATE if >SDR / high value
CL-12

Art 12 Further compensation

National-law further claims; EC261 sums deducted from Montreal awards.

→ Quantum interaction · U-11
CL-13

PTR / Package Travel

PTD 2015/2302 may govern remedy routing alongside EC261/UK261.

→ Dual regime screen
CL-14 · U-14

Art 13 Recourse

Preserve claims against ATC, handlers, OEM, airport. Flag early.

→ Limitation risk · U-14
CL-15 · U-15

ADR / Litigation

AviationADR ≤£15k · small claims ≤£10k · Part 36 above.

→ Forum & costs strategy
▼ EXTRAORDINARY CIRCUMSTANCES
04 · U-7 Extraordinary circumstances gate

U-7 — Wallentin-Hermann both limbs (per causal-chain event)

Apply independently to each event in the chain. Failure of either limb defeats EC for that event. Then proceed to U-8 reasonable measures.

Limb 1 — Inherency Circumstance not inherent in the normal exercise of the carrier’s activity.
Limb 2 — Control Circumstance beyond the carrier’s actual control on the facts of this flight.

Authority: Wallentin-Hermann v Alitalia C-549/07 · Pešková C-315/15 · then → U-8

▼ VERDICTS
08 · Six possible verdicts

Decide outcomes

Issued only after universal nodes, claim type, U-7/U-8, and evidence completeness are assessed.

DEFEND

EC established, chain holds, RM satisfied, evidence complete.

DEFEND WITH CONDITIONS

EC established but conditions first — evidence pulls, Art 9/8 gaps, or Pešková offset remainder.

SETTLE

EC fails / chain broken / RM failure / evidence gaps.

CONCEDE

Own-staff strike, crew illness, routine technical, denied boarding.

JUDGMENT REQUIRED

Ambiguous chain — intervening cause, concurrent causes, or DT-20 wake rule.

ESCALATE

Montreal >SDR, novel point, group action.
